Due diligence is where good deals are confirmed and bad deals are caught. This checklist covers the essential items every buyer should verify before closing on a business acquisition.

## Financial Due Diligence

- Three years of profit and loss statements
- Three years of business tax returns
- Balance sheet and accounts receivable/payable aging
- Bank statements to verify reported revenue
- Add-back schedule for owner discretionary expenses
- Monthly financials to spot seasonality

## Legal Due Diligence

- Business entity documents and good standing
- All contracts: customers, suppliers, leases
- Outstanding litigation or liens
- Licenses and permits required to operate
- Intellectual property and trademarks
- Employee agreements and any non-competes

## Operational Due Diligence

- Documented standard operating procedures
- Key employee roles and retention risk
- Equipment condition and maintenance records
- Inventory verification
- Software, systems, and technology stack

## Commercial Due Diligence

- Customer concentration analysis
- Customer retention and satisfaction
- Competitive landscape
- Market trends affecting the business
- Supplier reliability and pricing stability

## The Transition Plan

- Confirm the owner's training commitment in writing
- Identify which relationships need a personal handoff
- Plan for customer and employee communication post-sale

## The Bottom Line

Thorough due diligence protects your investment and gives you the knowledge to operate confidently from day one. Don't rush it — and lean on an accountant and attorney for the financial and legal portions.
