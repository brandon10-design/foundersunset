When you sit down with a retiring owner, the questions you ask reveal as much as the answers you get. Here are ten that cut to the heart of whether a business is right for you.

## 1. Why are you really retiring now?

You want to confirm the sale is genuinely about retirement and not a hidden problem like a looming competitor or lost major customer.

## 2. What does a typical week look like for you?

This tells you how much the business depends on the owner personally — and how replaceable that role is.

## 3. Who are your top customers, and how concentrated is your revenue?

If one client represents 40% of revenue, that's a major risk you need to understand.

## 4. What would you do to grow if you were staying?

Retiring owners often know exactly where the untapped opportunities are. Their answer is a roadmap.

## 5. Which employees are essential, and will they stay?

Key staff can make or break a transition. Find out who matters and how they'll react to a sale.

## 6. What are your relationships with suppliers and vendors?

Confirm these relationships and pricing will transfer to you.

## 7. What's the biggest challenge the business faces?

Every business has one. A candid answer signals an honest seller.

## 8. How are the financials kept, and can I see three years?

Clean, verifiable financials are non-negotiable.

## 9. Would you be willing to provide training and stay on during a transition?

Most retiring owners will — and it dramatically de-risks your purchase.

## 10. Would you consider carrying some seller financing?

This reduces your upfront cash and signals the seller's confidence in the business.

## The Bottom Line

Asking these questions early saves you from costly surprises later — and shows the retiring owner you're a serious, thoughtful buyer worthy of their legacy.
